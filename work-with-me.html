Process

How it works

1
Tell me what is broken
Email or book a call. Describe the problem as specifically as you can. The more detail the better, but if you just know something is slow or wrong and cannot name it yet, that is fine too.
2
Scope and proposal
I will map out what the project actually involves, what the deliverable looks like, and what it will take to get there. No vague retainer, no scope creep by default. Just a clear project with a clear output.
3
Build it
I work independently with check-ins at the stages that matter. You do not need to be in every step. You do need to be reachable when a decision has to be made.
4
Hand it off with documentation
Everything gets documented before it leaves my hands. The goal is a system that works without me in it. If it breaks six months later because nobody knows how it runs, that is a bad handoff.

FAQ

Common questions

How do you price projects?
Project-based. Scope and price are agreed upfront so there are no surprises at the end. Get in touch with a description of what you need and I will put together a proposal.
How long do projects typically take?
Depends entirely on scope. A single automation build or a focused audit can be done in a week or two. A full operations cleanup or SharePoint infrastructure build takes longer. Timeline is part of the proposal conversation.
Do you work with clients outside the US?
Yes. Everything is remote. Time zone overlap helps for check-ins but is not a hard requirement for most project types.
What if I am not sure exactly what I need?
That is a normal place to start. Book a call and describe the problem. Figuring out what the actual project should be is part of the work. You do not need to have it scoped before reaching out.
Will I be able to maintain what you build without you?
That is the goal. Every project includes documentation. If I build something you cannot run without calling me every month, I did not finish the job.
